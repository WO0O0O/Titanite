## SECTION 1 — WHICH AI INFRA BOTTLENECK DOES IT SOLVE?

* **Score: 1 / 1**
* **Evidence Quality:** Strong

AIRJ addresses the severe water consumption and power bottlenecks associated with AI data centre cooling. 100 kW+ racks require dense liquid cooling. Liquid cooling systems reject heat to the outside air, often using evaporative cooling towers that consume millions of gallons of water daily per site. Municipalities (e.g., in Virginia, Arizona, and the Middle East) are increasingly restricting data centre water allocations, creating a hard physical bottleneck to cluster scaling.

AirJoule solves this by harvesting pure water directly from the atmosphere while performing closed-loop dehumidification. Crucially, the process runs on low-grade waste heat (utilising server exhaust heat under 60°C) rather than relying on grid power. This eliminates the water consumption bottleneck and reduces cooling-related power requirements, directly lowering data centre Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE) metrics.
